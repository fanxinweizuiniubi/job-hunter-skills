# STAR Framework Guide

## Core Principles

- Use STAR to structure behavioral answers: **Situation → Task → Action → Result**
- Keep total answer to **2-3 minutes** when spoken (~250-350 words)
- Always draw from **real resume experiences** — never fabricate
- Emphasize **"I"** statements in Action section while acknowledging teamwork
- Prioritize **quantifiable results** and **personal impact**

## Detailed Breakdown

### Situation (10-15%)
Set the context concisely.

**Include:** Company/team/project, timeframe, key challenge, your role.  
**Avoid:** Long company history, unnecessary jargon, vague statements.

### Task (10%)
Clearly state your specific responsibility.

**Include:** Your goal, constraints, what was at stake.  
**Avoid:** Using "we" or leaving the task implied.

### Action (50% — the core)
Detail **what you personally did**, step by step.

**Include:** 
- Specific steps and sequence
- Technical decisions + reasoning
- How you handled obstacles
- Collaboration (while highlighting your contribution)

**Avoid:** 
- Vague team actions
- Skipping difficult parts
- Actions without explaining "why"

### Result (20-25%)
Quantify the outcome and reflect.

**Include:** 
- Metrics (%, $, time, users, scale)
- Business impact
- Lessons learned or recognition
- How it influenced future work

**Avoid:** Vague success statements, taking full credit for team outcomes.

## Good vs Bad Examples

**Good STAR (Concise & Powerful):**
> "At Acme Corp, our payment system was at risk of failing during Black Friday (Situation). My task was to scale it to handle 10x load within 10 days (Task). I profiled queries, implemented Redis caching and async processing, defended the changes with security, and rolled it out with feature flags (Action). The system handled peak traffic with zero downtime, latency dropped 82%, and the pattern was adopted team-wide (Result)."

**Bad STAR:**
> "We had a problem with the system. The team fixed it and everything worked out."

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

1. **The "We" Trap** → Replace most "we" with "I" + teamwork context
2. **Vague Results** → Always push for numbers and business impact
3. **Action Too Short** → Ensure Action is the longest section
4. **Rambling Situation** → Limit to 1-2 sentences
5. **No Reflection** → Especially important for failure questions
6. **Generic Stories** → Tie tightly to candidate’s unique experience

## STAR Confidence Rating

- **Strong**: Clear metrics, strong "I" actions, direct match to competency
- **Moderate**: Relevant experience but weak metrics or indirect match
- **Weak**: Limited evidence — suggest reframing or acknowledge gap honestly

## Special Case Adaptations

- **Failure Questions**: Spend more on lessons learned and subsequent improvements
- **Conflict Questions**: Stay factual, avoid blame, focus on resolution process
- **Leadership Questions**: Highlight influence without formal authority
- **Learning Quickly Questions**: Emphasize learning process and rapid application

**Usage in Interview Coach:**
When generating STAR answers in Phase 3, always:
1. Map to specific resume bullet
2. Rate confidence (Strong/Moderate/Weak)
3. Provide the full STAR response + key takeaway
