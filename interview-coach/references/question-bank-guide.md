# Question Bank Guide

## Core Generation Principles

- All questions must be **personalized** using the JD + Resume cross-analysis from `extraction-criteria.md`
- Prioritize areas with tension/gaps
- Calibrate difficulty strictly to seniority level
- For behavioral questions: Always suggest relevant resume experience when possible
- Aim for 25-40 questions total, balanced across categories

## 1. Role-Specific Technical Questions (5-10)

Calibrate by seniority:

**Junior:** Fundamentals, basic implementation, tool familiarity  
**Mid-level:** System design, trade-offs, optimization, practical troubleshooting  
**Senior:** Architecture, migration, mentoring + technical, retrospective thinking  
**Staff/Principal:** Org-scale design, cross-team influence, technical strategy, measurement frameworks

**Generation Tip:** Base questions on explicit JD requirements and resume technologies.

## 2. Behavioral Questions (8-12)

Map JD soft skills to these categories:

- **Leadership & Ownership**
- **Conflict Resolution & Feedback**
- **Failure, Learning & Resilience**
- **Teamwork & Collaboration**
- **Prioritization & Execution**
- **Stakeholder Management**
- **Customer/User Impact**

**Best Practice:** For each behavioral question, recommend 1-2 relevant resume experiences to use as STAR stories.

## 3. Resume Deep-Dive Questions (5-8)

Target:
- Career transitions and motivations
- Specific achievements (especially metrics)
- Role in "we" projects
- Skill depth and recency
- Gaps and short tenures
- Long-term career narrative

## 4. Situational / Hypothetical Questions (3-5)

Create scenarios directly from JD responsibilities and common challenges in the target role/domain.

Examples by role type (Engineering / Product / Data / Management) are provided in the original version — use them as templates.

## 5. Company-Specific Questions (3-5)

- Why this company?
- Understanding of their mission, recent news
