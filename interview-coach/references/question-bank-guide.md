# Question Bank Guide

## Question Taxonomy

### 1. Role-Specific Technical Questions

Generate questions based on the core technical requirements in the JD. Calibrate difficulty to seniority.

**Junior-level patterns:**
- "What is X and how does it work?" (fundamental knowledge)
- "Explain the difference between X and Y." (comparative understanding)
- "Walk me through how you would build Z." (basic implementation)
- "What is your experience with [tool from JD]?" (tool familiarity)

**Mid-level patterns:**
- "How would you design a system for [requirement from JD]?" (system design)
- "What trade-offs would you consider when choosing between X and Y for [use case]?" (trade-off analysis)
- "Describe a time you had to optimize [system/process]. What was your approach?" (optimization)
- "How do you handle [common challenge in this role]?" (practical problem-solving)

**Senior-level patterns:**
- "Design a scalable [system type] that handles [scale metric from JD]." (architecture)
- "How would you migrate from [legacy system] to [modern system] without downtime?" (migration strategy)
- "Your team is struggling with [problem]. How would you approach it?" (leadership + technical)
- "What would you do differently if you had to rebuild [project from resume] today?" (retrospective + growth)

**Staff/Principal-level patterns:**
- "How would you design [system] for an org of [size] with [constraint]?" (org-scale architecture)
- "Two teams disagree on [technical decision]. How do you resolve it?" (cross-org influence)
- "What technical bets would you make for [company's domain] in the next 3 years?" (strategic thinking)
- "How would you measure the success of [technical initiative]?" (impact measurement)

### 2. Behavioral Questions

Map JD soft skills to standard behavioral question frameworks:

**Leadership / Ownership:**
- "Tell me about a time you took ownership of a project that wasn't going well."
- "Describe a situation where you had to lead without formal authority."
- "Tell me about a time you challenged a decision or pushed back on a stakeholder."

**Conflict Resolution:**
- "Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a teammate. How did you resolve it?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to deliver difficult feedback."
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's approach."

**Failure / Growth:**
- "Tell me about a project that failed. What happened and what did you learn?"
- "Describe a time you made a significant mistake. How did you handle it?"
- "Tell me about feedback you received that was hard to hear. What did you do with it?"

**Teamwork / Collaboration:**
- "Tell me about a time you had to work with a difficult teammate."
- "Describe a cross-functional project you worked on. How did you align stakeholders?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to onboard or mentor someone."

**Prioritization / Time Management:**
- "Tell me about a time you had multiple competing deadlines. How did you prioritize?"
- "Describe a situation where you had to cut scope to meet a deadline."
- "Tell me about a time you had to say no to a request."

**Problem-Solving:**
- "Tell me about the most challenging technical problem you've solved."
- "Describe a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "Tell me about a time you had to debug an issue that was hard to reproduce."

**Customer / Impact Focus:**
- "Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer or user."
- "Describe a time you identified a user pain point and addressed it."
- "Tell me about how you measure the impact of your work."

### 3. Resume Deep-Dive Questions

Generate questions that naturally arise from reading the resume:

**From job titles and tenures:**
- "I see you went from [Role A] to [Role B]. What motivated that transition?"
- "You were at [Company] for [X years]. What was your biggest accomplishment there?"
- "I notice there's a gap between [Date A] and [Date B]. Can you tell me about that period?"

**From achievements:**
- "You mentioned [achievement with metric]. Can you walk me through how you achieved that?"
- "What was your specific contribution to [project]? It says 'we' — what was your individual role?"
- "That [project] sounds impressive. What would you do differently if you did it again?"

**From skills and technologies:**
- "I see you listed [skill]. How have you used it in practice?"
- "You've worked with [tech A] and [tech B]. Which do you prefer and why?"
- "How do you keep up with [technology/domain] given how fast it changes?"

**From career trajectory:**
- "Your roles seem to be getting more [technical/managerial]. Was that intentional?"
- "I see you've worked in [industry A] and [industry B]. What drew you to each?"
- "What are you looking for in your next role that you don't have now?"

### 4. Situational / Case Questions

Create hypothetical scenarios based on JD responsibilities:

**For engineering roles:**
- "Your production system is down. Walk me through your debugging process."
- "A critical feature is behind schedule. The PM wants to cut testing. What do you do?"
- "You're asked to add a feature that would introduce significant technical debt. How do you respond?"

**For product roles:**
- "A key stakeholder wants to add a feature that doesn't align with the product roadmap. How do you handle it?"
- "Your metrics show user engagement dropping. What do you investigate first?"
- "You have to choose between two features with equal business value but different engineering costs. How do you decide?"

**For data roles:**
- "A business leader challenges your analysis. The data doesn't support their intuition. How do you handle it?"
- "You find a critical data quality issue that affects past reports. What do you do?"
- "How would you design an experiment to measure [business metric from JD]?"

**For management roles:**
- "A high-performing team member is disengaged. What do you do?"
- "Two teams are blocking each other. How do you resolve it?"
- "You need to deliver bad news about a project delay to leadership. How do you approach it?"

### 5. Company-Specific Questions

Research the company (if possible) and generate:

**Mission and values alignment:**
- "Why do you want to work at [Company] specifically?"
- "How do you align with [Company]'s mission of [mission statement]?"
- "What do you think about [Company]'s recent [product launch / strategic direction]?"

**Product / domain knowledge:**
- "What do you think are the biggest challenges [Company] faces in [domain]?"
- "How would you improve [Company]'s [product/service]?"
- "Who do you think are [Company]'s main competitors and how do we differentiate?"

**Culture-specific:**
- "What kind of environment do you work best in?" (compare to known culture)
- "How do you feel about [known company practice, e.g., 'writing detailed docs', 'working in-person']?"

### 6. Questions to Ask the Interviewer

Help the candidate prepare thoughtful questions that show engagement and help them evaluate the role:

**About the role:**
- "What does success look like in this role in the first 90 days?"
- "What is the biggest challenge the person in this role will face?"
- "How does this role interact with [team X] and [team Y]?"

**About the team:**
- "How is the team structured and where would I fit in?"
- "What is the team's current biggest priority?"
- "How does the team handle disagreements about technical decisions?"

**About growth:**
- "What does the career path look like for this role?"
- "What kind of mentorship or learning opportunities are available?"
- "How does the company support professional development?"

**About culture and work style:**
- "How would you describe the team culture in three words?"
- "What do you enjoy most about working here?"
- "How does the company balance innovation with execution?"

**About the interview process:**
- "What are the next steps in the process?"
- "Is there anything about my background that concerns you?"
- "What are you looking for in an ideal candidate that we haven't covered?"
